Lasagnes

Description

Lasagna is an Italian dish made of layers of pasta, tomato sauce, ground meat (or vegetables), béchamel, and melted cheese. Baked in the oven, it’s loved for its rich flavors and comforting, cheesy texture.

  • Lasagna pasta sheets
  • Ground meat (beef, pork, or a mix)
  • Tomato sauce (or passata)
  • Onion (chopped)
  • Garlic (chopped or minced)
  • Béchamel sauce (milk, butter, flour, salt, pepper, nutmeg)
  • Grated cheese (mozzarella, parmesan, or a mix)
  • Olive oil (for sautéing the meat and vegetables)
  • Herbs (basil, oregano, thyme)
  • Salt and pepper (for seasoning)

steps for preparing lasagna

  1. Prepare the béchamel sauce: Melt butter in a saucepan, add flour, then gradually pour in milk while stirring until thickened. Season with salt, pepper, and nutmeg.
  2. Cook the meat: Sauté chopped onion and garlic in olive oil, then add the ground meat. Cook until the meat is browned.
  3. Add the tomato sauce: Stir in the tomato sauce (or passata) with the meat, season with herbs (basil, oregano), and let it simmer.
  4. Prepare the lasagna pasta: If using dry pasta, cook it in boiling salted water according to the package instructions. If using no-boil sheets, skip this step.
  5. Assemble the lasagna: In a baking dish, layer the pasta, meat sauce, béchamel, and grated cheese.
  6. Bake: Top with more grated cheese and bake in a preheated oven at 180°C (350°F) for about 30-40 minutes, until the top is golden and bubbling.
  7. Let it rest: Let the lasagna rest for a few minutes before cutting and serving.
